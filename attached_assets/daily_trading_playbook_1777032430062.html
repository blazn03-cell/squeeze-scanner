Daily Trading Playbook

Hour-by-hour, entry to exit. Follow word-for-word for 10 trading days before changing anything.

PRE-MARKET

The scanner does the work. You stay calm.

8:30 AM
Scanner Fires (You're Not Doing Anything)

What's happening: Replit runs your first scan automatically. Data lands in Google Sheet.

What you do: Nothing. You might still be asleep. That's fine.

Why: The system works without you. That's the whole point.

8:35 AM
Phone Check (60 seconds)

What to do:

  • Grab phone
  • Open Google Sheets app → UW Scanner Log → Today tab
  • Look at the summary strip at top

What you're looking for:

  • Any candidates at all? → Trading day possible
  • Zero candidates? → Slow day, don't force anything
  • Error row? → Something broke, check Replit Deployments later
Decision
Go back to sleep or start your morning. Don't react yet. Too early.
9:00 AM
Scanner Fires Again (Be at Your Desk)

What's happening: Second scheduled scan runs. More data lands.

What you do: Be at your desk. Coffee ready. Computer on. TradingView open.

Why: You need 20 minutes to prepare before the bell. Don't start prep at 9:25.

THE BRIEF

Three minutes that set up the entire trading day.

9:20 AM
The Brief (3 minutes, Do This Every Day)

Open Google Sheet on desktop → Today tab.

Step 1 (30 sec) — Read the summary strip:

  • How many total candidates?
  • How many scored 80+?
  • Any triple hits (all 3 scans)?
  • What's the top ticker?

Step 2 (60 sec) — Scan the rows:

  • Dark green score (80+) = priority
  • Yellow score (60–79) = maybe
  • Green Hit Count of 3 = highest conviction
  • Purple flow = institutional money confirmed

Step 3 (60 sec) — Click Repeat Hits tab:

  • Any tickers here? These are your A-list.
  • Usually 0–3 names show up
  • Read the Latest Thesis for each

Step 4 (30 sec) — Pick your plan:

  • Maximum 2 tickers to work today. Not 3. Not 5. Two.
  • Write ticker, entry zone, stop zone, one-sentence thesis
Zero Strong Candidates?
Close the sheet. Don't trade today. Skipping bad days is how you print good months.
9:25 AM
Scanner Fires Last Time

What's happening: Final pre-market scan. Fresh data appears.

What you do: Refresh the Today tab. Check if your 2 picks still rank high.

  • Dropped off? → Question them
  • Climbed higher? → Conviction confirmed

WAIT WINDOW

Market opens but you don't trade. This is where discipline is built.

9:30 AM
Market Opens — HANDS OFF

Rule: No entries between 9:30 and 10:00 AM. Period.

What to do during this 30 minutes:

  • Open TradingView for your 2 picks
  • Apply studies: Ichimoku Cloud, Squeeze Pro, MFI
  • Set the ORB 15-min study on each chart
  • Check VIX (top of TradingView)
  • Check if any macro news broke overnight
VIX Decision Tree
  • VIX under 20 → full position size planned
  • VIX 20–25 → half position size
  • VIX over 25 → skip the day entirely. Close charts. Don't fight regime.
Macro Kill Switches — Cancel If:
  • Major geopolitical headline hit overnight
  • Surprise Fed announcement
  • Ticker gapped against your thesis
  • Options bid-ask wider than 10% of premium
Do Not
Place any trade. Not a small one. Not a test one. None. Do not add new tickers. Do not change entry/stop levels.
9:45 AM
Mark the Opening Range

On each TradingView chart:

  • Note the highest price the first 15 minutes hit
  • Note the lowest price the first 15 minutes hit
  • This is your ORB (Opening Range Breakout) zone

Why: You're about to trade the break of this range. Price closing above the high = confirmed bullish momentum. Price rejecting it = setup failed, don't enter.

Action
Watch. Just watch. Don't click buy.

EXECUTION WINDOW

All 13 green or skip. No exceptions.

10:00 AM
Run the 13-Point Checklist

Scanner Already Confirmed (7 points):

  • Dark pool accumulation
  • Short interest elevated
  • Bullish options flow
  • No earnings next 7 days
  • GEX cluster above
  • Real premium size
  • Multi-scan conviction

You Manually Confirm (6 points):

  • VIX regime acceptable (under 25)
  • Price broke above ORB 15-min high and holding
  • Price above Ichimoku cloud
  • Squeeze Pro momentum bars green and firing
  • MFI above 50 and rising
  • No macro headline risk in next 24 hours
Decision Rule
All 13 green = enter. 12 green, 1 missing = skip. No exceptions. No "close enough."
10:00–10:30 AM
Entry (If All 13 Green)

Choose ONE structure:

Structure A — Call Spread (Grind, VIX 15–22)
  • Buy strike at or 1 above current price
  • Sell strike 3–5 above that
  • Expiration: 2–3 weeks out
  • Lower theta risk, hedged by short leg
Structure B — Naked Call (Clear Ignition, VIX Under 18)
  • Buy ATM or 1 strike OTM
  • Expiration: 2–3 weeks out
  • Higher reward, higher theta risk

Position size:

  • VIX under 20: full normal size
  • VIX 20–25: half size
  • VIX over 25: skip the day
Verify Before Clicking Buy
  • Open interest on strike > 500
  • Bid-ask spread less than 10% of premium
  • Volume today already exceeds OI
  • You're within the entry_zone price from dashboard

Click buy. Confirm fill. Screenshot the entry.

POSITION MANAGEMENT

Let the trade work. Don't hover. Don't chase.

10:30 AM – 12:00 PM
Position Management

What to do:

  • Watch price action on your tickers
  • Do NOT stare at P&L constantly
  • Let the trade breathe

What you're watching for:

  • Price continuing toward GEX target → good
  • Price stalling at ORB high without pushing → warning
  • Price breaking back below ORB high → exit warning
Do Not
Add to positions. Roll strikes. Open new positions on other tickers. Check scanner for more ideas.

Why: The morning signal is the morning signal. Adding exposure mid-session = chasing.

12:00 – 2:00 PM
Lunch Chop (Stay Disciplined)

Reality: Volume dies. Algorithms dominate. Sloppy price action. Most intraday reversals happen here.

What to do:

  • Set price alerts at your stop_zone and target_zone
  • Walk away from the screen if possible
  • Eat lunch. Take a walk. Don't hover.
If Stop Triggers
Exit immediately. Full position. Market order if needed on options. No "let me see if it bounces back." Stop means stop.
If First Target Hits (+75%)
Sell 50% of position. Move stop on remaining to breakeven. Now you're playing with house money.
2:00 – 3:30 PM
Power Hour Prep (Re-engage)

Check your position:

  • Still above entry, above ORB high, above Ichimoku cloud? → Hold
  • Any of those broken? → Start planning exit

GEX target check:

  • Price approaching GEX cluster target? → Prepare to scale out more
  • Not there yet but still trending up? → Hold remaining 30%

Trailing stop:

  • Use Ichimoku Kijun line as trailing stop on final 20%
  • Price closes below Kijun on 15-min chart → exit

CLOSE DECISION

Lock profits. Limit overnight risk.

3:30 – 4:00 PM
Close Decision

The big question: Hold overnight or close out?

Close the Position If ANY Of:
  • At +100% gain or better on any portion → lock profits, theta kills weekend holds
  • VIX spiked during the day → reduce overnight risk
  • Macro headline risk tomorrow (Fed, econ data, geopolitics)
  • Price stalling and momentum faded
  • Not confident enough to hold through the night
Hold Overnight ONLY If ALL Of:
  • Scanner dashboard showed triple-hit conviction
  • Dark pool data confirms ongoing accumulation
  • No earnings or macro catalysts in next 48 hours
  • Ichimoku daily chart still bullish
  • VIX stable and under 20

Default rule: When in doubt, close. Overnight holds on options during volatile regimes = binary risk.

4:00 PM
Market Closes

Immediate actions:

  • Confirm all intended exits actually filled
  • Screenshot your P&L for the day
  • Do not touch the scanner or charts
4:00 – 5:00 PM
Journal (5 minutes)

Open a note. Write:

  • What tickers did the scanner surface?
  • What did I trade?
  • What happened?
  • What would I change?
Why This Matters
This is the single most important habit. Without it, you can't tune the scanner and you can't improve your discipline.
After 5 PM
Done

What you do: Close Replit. Close Google Sheets. Close TradingView.

What you do NOT do: Research tomorrow's trades tonight. That's Sunday's job.

Why: Mental separation matters. Traders who think about the market 14 hours a day burn out and make revenge trades the next morning.

THE 5 EXIT TRIGGERS

Any ONE fires = exit. Memorize these.

1
🛑 Stop Zone Hit
When: Price touches stop_zone from dashboard
Action: Exit entire position immediately
No hesitation: Market order on options if needed
Why: You defined this at 9:20 AM with a clear head. Your 2 PM brain doesn't get to veto your 9 AM brain.
2
🎯 Target Hit (+75%)
When: Position up 75% or more
Action: Sell 50% of position
Move stop: Remaining 50% stop now at breakeven
Why: Lock half the profit. Now the trade is free.
3
🎯 GEX Target Hit
When: Price reaches GEX cluster zone (target from dashboard)
Action: Sell another 30% of position
Remaining 20%: Trail with Ichimoku Kijun line
Why: GEX cluster is where market maker hedging accelerates — and where selling pressure kicks in. Take money at the magnet.
4
⏰ 2-Day Time Stop
When: 2 trading days have passed and thesis hasn't fired
Action: Exit regardless of P&L
Why: Theta is already eating you alive. Squeezes fire fast or not at all. A setup that didn't ignite in 2 days won't ignite.
5
🚨 Regime Change
When: VIX spikes above 25 intraday, or major macro headline breaks
Action: Exit all options positions immediately
Why: Your entry was based on one regime. The regime changed. The trade's assumptions are invalid.

Exit Priority Waterfall

When multiple signals fire at once, obey in this order:

  1. Stop zone — always first, no exceptions
  2. Regime change — protects capital when macro breaks
  3. Time stop — protects from theta death
  4. Target hits — lock profits in sequence
  5. Trail stop — let winners run until trend breaks
